*A list of concepts that are either new or done differently than in Dungeons and Dragons 5e*

**Free Action**
A free action is a quick action anyone can do during another character’s action or at any other time (warn somebody verbally, speak two short sentences, drop an item or a weapon, etc.). Characters can do two free actions in a round. They may do more, but it uses an action to do so.

**Attack Bonus**
For melee attacks, the character adds their Strength modifier, and for ranged attacks, the character adds their Dexterity modifier. They also add any attack- appropriate bonuses to the attack.

**Blindness**
If a character finds themselves blinded, possibly by an injury, or fighting in darkness without light, the character suffers a -4 penalty to all attack rolls, cannot make Spot checks and moves at half Move.

**Called Shot**
When declaring a ranged attack on a specific body part or target, there is a -3 penalty for targeting a specific area.

**Charge**
You must use all your actions in a turn to charge an opponent. You may charge up to a distance of Move x10 in feet. You must have a clear, strait path to the opponent. If there is anything hindering the path (debris, oil, bodies, etc.) the attacker must make a DEX check or trip on the way. The charger gains +3 to hit and gets double their STR modifier in damage (or a +1 if they have a negative STR modifier). If the charge attack hits and is at least 10 higher than the defender’s AC, the defender is knocked back 10’.

*Natural 1* - I can make you roll on the Critical Fumble table

**Defensive**
As an action, you can help defend another character. You cannot attack that round, and the character you are defending gets +4 to AC, +1 to all Saves, and +1 to all Skill rolls for as long as you are defending them. If the person you are defending is hit, you have a 50% chance of splitting the damage with them.

**Fearless**
The character or monster is immune to Fear and Terror. They automatically pass all Courage saves and Terror checks and cannot be intimidated.

**Grab**
A grab attempt can be made with arms, a tentacle, or other grasping limbs. Make a melee attack against the target. If successful, the target makes a STR check adding the STR modifier of the attacker to their roll. If they pass the STR check, they avoid the grab. If they fail, they are grabbed. While grabbed, they lose all actions except speaking and trying to break free. Once a round, they may attempt to break free with another STR check as detailed above. Certain situations may add Advantage or Disadvantage to the attempt to break free of a grab; see the Grab Modifiers table below. Anytime the grabber takes damage, the character being grabbed may attempt a STR check (as detailed above) to escape, subtracting the damage taken by the grabber from their roll to escape.

**Ignited**
Anything on fire takes 1 cumulative point of additional damage at the beginning of each round until extinguished (i.e., 1, then 2, then 3, etc.). If they come into contact with any other beings while on fire, the other being must make a DEX check or catch fire as well. You may take an action to try to extinguish yourself or someone else by making a DEX check.

**Improvised Weapons**
If you use an improvised weapon (broken bottle, a stick, trash can lid, a handful of coins, etc.) you attack at -3, unless you have training.

**Initiative**
Initiative is rolled on a d6, adding in any bonus (from DEX or otherwise), with the highest total winning the Initiative. If there is a tie, the character with the highest DEX goes first. If they are still tied, the character with the highest Move goes first.

**Invisibility**
If you act upon a target while invisible, the target is now effectively blind when interacting with the attacker while they remain invisible.

**Knockback**
When a character is knocked back, they take an additional point of damage for every 10’ they go. If they hit another solid item or character, they take an additional d4 damage, as does the object they struck.

**Listen**
Listen checks are made when a character attempts to identify or notice a sound (a secret whisper, a far-off scream, somebody trying to stealth by, etc.). A character adds their WIS modifier to their check roll.

**Madness**
For every point of Madness you accumulate, you come closer to losing your mind. Madness points are permanent, but they can be removed through some mystical and esoteric means. If your Madness exceeds your Wisdom score at any time, you are forever lost, your mind having shattered.

*Madness Effects*
As a character begins to gain Madness points, things begin to happen to them. Their minds become corrupted by unknown, terrifying forces. If an effect causes a character’s Madness to decrease*, they lose the equivalent Madness effects.

**Phobia**
When a character encounters one of their fears, they must make a Courage check at Disadvantage, or they must roll on the Failed Courage/Terror Effect Table
Pass or fail, the character feels uncomfortable within 100’ of their fear, giving them -1 to all attacks, -1 to all skill checks and -2 to their Mental Save stat.

**Move**
Standard Movement rates refer to a character’s ability to move around on foot, in both combat and non-combat rates. Characters start with a Move of 12 on land and 9 swimming, unless otherwise noted. A character’s Move x10 indicates the maximum feet they can move in a full round. The table below lists a characters Movement rates under different conditions (i.e., a character that has a Move of 15 can move a total of 150' normally in a round, 300' while running, 30' while crawling, or 50' and still be able to make a normal attack). If a character moves more than 1/3 of their Move but does not run, they may still attack at -4. A character may move up to 1/3 of their Move, rounded up, and still attack without penalty. A character may run and attack at a penalty, losing half of their total attack bonuses (rounded down), and they double any ROF penalties they incur.

**Outsmart**
When successfully attacked or affected by an ability/ spell of a hostile character or creature, the character may attempt to outsmart their foe (before damage is rolled). Roll a d20 and add your INT and WIS modifiers and any outsmart bonus to the roll. If the outsmart roll is equal to or greater than the attack roll, then roll on the chart below, and they may switch places in Initiative with the attacker if they so choose. If the attack or effect had no initial roll, then
the difficulty you must beat is equal to a d20 roll plus any modifiers the GM sets (INT and WIS modifiers and their own outsmart bonus if applicable).] A character with outsmart can use it equal to their INT + WIS modifiers per day.

**Prone**
If a Character attacks a Prone victim, the victim loses all bonuses to Armor Class and suffers a -4 Armor Class penalty.

**Ranged Attacks**
You can attempt to shoot past the given range at a penalty. Attacks under the range are rolled as normal. Attacks from the maximum range to double range are rolled at -6. Ranged attacks made at 5' or closer are at Disadvantage (or if it is within reach of your foe).
If you draw a ranged weapon and attack in the same action, your attack is at -3. If you shoot into a crowd of three or more, your attack is at -3.

**Rate of Fire (ROF)**
When firing an automatic or energy weapon that has a ROF of more than 1, you can choose to shoot once or to shoot multiple rounds up to, or equal to, the ROF at once. If you shoot once, roll your attack
as normal. If you unload more than one shot at once, you must roll to attack with each shot. The first shot is at -2 to attack, the second is at -4, the third is at -6, and any beyond that are at -8.

**Reloading**
Reloading a weapon takes a single action

**Seize the Moment**
Some characters have the ability to seize the moment in combat by taking advantage of an opponent’s mistakes. They can take those mistakes and turn them into an extra action. A character with seize the moment may immediately use a bonus attack against an opponent that does one of the actions below within your reach. You can only seize the moment once a day for each bonus point you have in it.
• Is interacting with a device or item in preparation
• Has just rolled a critical fumble
• Ends the round stunned
• End your turn with them unaware of you
• They fall or are tripped
• They get Knocked Back
• They run away from you (make a run Move)
• Shoot a gun or bow in melee

**Surprise Attack**
Add d4 of damage.

**Thrown Weapons**
Each weapon has a listed range, but you can throw it further by taking a negative attack modifier. You receive a -2 to hit for each extra 10’. A high STR can increase the range of thrown weapons. You add 10’ to the base range of a thrown weapon for each point of positive STR modifier a character has.

**Tripping**
You may try to trip someone that is next to your character or within range of a reach weapon. Make an attack against your target. If you hit, the target makes a DEX check adding your STR modifier to their roll. If they pass, nothing happens. If they fail, they become tripped and are prone until their next action. If you try to trip someone more than 6” + taller than you, you roll your attack at Disadvantage, and the target rolls their DEX check at Advantage.

**XP**

*Session Survival*
The first way you earn XP. 1 XP is given simply for completing or surviving an entire gaming session. Even if your character does not get much accomplished during the gaming session, this XP is still earned.
*Encounter XP*
This can be earned up to three times in one gaming session. 1 Encounter XP is earned by being involved in a combat scenario. If there are three separate combat scenarios in one gaming session,
then 3 XP are earned. If there are five separate combat scenarios, still only 3 XP are earned as that is the maximum for a session. NOTE: Encounter XP is rewarded regardless, win or lose.

*Exceptional Roleplaying*
This can be earned one time per session for 1 XP. This XP is given to players that goes above and beyond in the department of roleplaying. 

*Discretionary XP*
Is earned up to twice a gaming session for a maximum of 2 XP awarded at the GM’s discretion. This can be given for things like solving a difficult puzzle, overcoming the odds, or achieving something due to luck.

*Hero XP*
This is given to a single player that performs a selfless act of valor. This act must be something that is above and beyond the goal of the session. Unlike the other types of XP that can be earned, Hero XP can only be earned by 1 player, per gaming session, and if nothing heroic is done, then no player earns the Hero XP.

Maximum XP per session:
Session Survival: 1 XP per player
Encounter: 3 XP per player
Exceptional Roleplaying: 1 XP per player
Discretionary: 2 XP per player
Hero: 1 XP for 1 player NOTE: Using this method,
all Character Classes level at the same pace.
